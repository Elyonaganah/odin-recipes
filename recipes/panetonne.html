Panetonne

Description

Indulge in the decadent delight of our exquisitely served panettone dish, a true symbol of Italian culinary craftsmanship. Served on a pristine porcelain platter, the panettone stands tall, its golden-brown crust adorned with a delicate dusting of powdered sugar, reminiscent of freshly fallen snow. As you slice into its soft, buttery interior, the air fills with a symphony of aromas—a tantalizing blend of citrus zest, fragrant vanilla, and hints of almond, teasing your senses with every bite.

Each slice reveals a mosaic of candied fruits and plump raisins, generously distributed throughout the velvety crumb, imparting bursts of sweetness and texture with every indulgent mouthful. Paired with a steaming cup of freshly brewed coffee or a glass of sweet Vin Santo, our panettone dish promises a truly magical culinary experience, perfect for celebrating cherished moments with loved ones or savoring quiet moments of solitude. Buon appetito!

Ingredients

  • Flour
  • Yeast
  • Sugar
  • Butter
  • Eggs
  • Milk
  • Vanilla
  • Lemon zest
  • Salt
  • Orange zest
  • Candied fruits (e.g., orange peel, citron, lemon peel)
  • Raisins

Steps

  1. Step 1: Activate the yeast:
      Dissolve the yeast in warm milk with a pinch of sugar. Let it sit for 5-10 minutes until frothy.
  2. Step 2: Prepare the dough:
      In a large mixing bowl, combine the activated yeast mixture with flour, sugar, softened butter, eggs, vanilla extract, lemon zest, orange zest, and a pinch of salt. Mix until a sticky dough forms.
  3. Step 3: Knead the dough:
      Transfer the dough to a floured surface and knead it for 10-15 minutes until smooth and elastic. Incorporate candied fruits and raisins into the dough during the last few minutes of kneading.
  4. Step 4: First rise:
      Place the dough in a lightly greased bowl, cover it, and let it rise in a warm, draft-free place for 1-2 hours or until doubled in size.
  5. Step 5: Shape the dough:
      Punch down the risen dough and shape it into a ball. Place it in a panettone mold or a tall, cylindrical pan lined with parchment paper. Make a small cross-shaped incision on top of the dough.
  6. Step 6: Second rise:
      Cover the shaped dough and let it rise again for 1-2 hours or until it doubles in size and reaches the top of the panettone mold.
  7. Step 7: Preheat the oven:
      Preheat the oven to the specified temperature (usually around 350°F or 180°C).
  8. Step 8: Bake:
      Bake the panettone in the preheated oven for 40-45 minutes or until golden brown and a skewer inserted into the center comes out clean.
  9. Step 9: Cool:
      Remove the panettone from the oven and let it cool completely on a wire rack before slicing and serving.

Enjoy your freshly baked panettone!
